# P1 Interview Script — Halastudy demand signal

> **Purpose:** Surface 5 verbatim quotes that tell us whether Halastudy's wedge — *quiet by default, camera is the signal, audio is the exception* — solves a problem GCC/Saudi students actually have. Not a pitch script. Listen mode only.

## Cohort
- 5 students × ~15 min each.
- GCC/Saudi, currently studying (uni / late high school / post-grad cert).
- Mix of "studies alone" vs "studies with friends" vs "tried Discord study rooms."
- WhatsApp / FaceTime is fine. Don't gate on video.

## Opening (≤30 sec)
> "I'm exploring how students in Saudi/Gulf study together, especially late nights. I'd love to learn from how *you* actually study — I'm not selling anything. 15 minutes? You can stop me anytime."

If they ask what you're building, deflect once:
> "Honestly still figuring it out — that's why I want to hear how you study first."

## Body — 5 questions, in order

### 1. Anchor on the most recent session
> "Walk me through the last time you studied for more than 30 minutes. Where were you? What time? Were other people around?"
- Listening for: alone vs with friends, in-person vs remote, dorm vs cafe vs home, night vs day.
- ⚠️ Don't lead toward "study apps."

### 2. The "studying with friends" pattern
> "When you study with friends — even informally — what does that actually look like? In-person? On a call?"
- If they say "I don't" → skip to Q4.
- If they say "on FaceTime / WhatsApp video / Discord" → **gold.** Probe: "Camera on? Mic on? Both? How does it usually start?"

### 3. The mic question (the wedge test, indirectly)
> "When you're on a study call with friends — is anyone actually talking, or is it mostly silent with the cameras on?"
- This is the single most diagnostic question. If they say "we mute almost the whole time, it's just nice to see each other" → the wedge is real for them.
- If they say "no, we chat the whole time" → they're a different segment; note and move on.

### 4. What breaks
> "What's the worst part of studying alone, or studying with friends on a call? What makes you stop?"
- Listening for: isolation, distraction, motivation, accountability, "I closed the call because of X."

### 5. The "if a magic genie" question
> "If a magic genie gave you the *perfect* way to study with one or two friends remotely, what would that actually feel like? No tech constraints."
- Listening for: their version of the product, in their words. Don't compare to what you're building. Just take notes.

## Close (≤1 min)
> "Two last things. One — do you know anyone else I should talk to about this? Two — if I built something based on what you said, would you try the beta?"
- Capture yes / maybe / no + their WhatsApp/IG/X handle.
- 2 referrals minimum if they say yes.

## What "green" looks like

≥3 out of 5 say something like *"we mute most of the time, it's just having each other on camera that helps"* — un-prompted. That's P1 green.

## What "yellow" looks like

1-2 wedge matches, mixed feedback, no one says "yes I'd try the beta." Note honestly, don't round up.

## What "red" looks like

Zero wedge matches across all 5. Or all 5 say "I'd never study on video, my room is messy, I hate cameras." → product hypothesis is wrong for this cohort, pivot conversation goes to Day 7 go/no-go.

## Tips
- Record audio if they're ok with it. Otherwise type during the call — they won't mind.
- Don't say "Pomodoro" — say "focus block" or use their word.
- If they say something interesting, ask "tell me more about that" and shut up.
- 30 min max per call including buffer. If a call hits gold, run long; the script is a floor not a ceiling.
