# Halastudy — Visual Brand & Theme Brief

**For:** Designer  
**From:** Aziz  
**Date:** May 14, 2026  
**Goal:** Develop a distinctive theme (color, typography, mood, motion language) for the MVP. Web + iOS/Android. Bilingual EN/AR with full RTL parity.

---

## 1. Product in one paragraph

Halastudy is a live-camera **body-doubling study app** for GCC students and young professionals. People drop into focus rooms with cameras on (mics off), start a Pomodoro, and work alongside others until they're done. It's not a classroom, not a Discord, not a video call — it's a *quiet study room* you can join 24/7. Think Focusmate × StudyStream, built for Arabic-speaking students first.

---

## 2. Who we're designing for

- **Primary:** GCC university students (KSA, UAE, Kuwait, Bahrain, Qatar, Oman), age 17–26. Med, eng, law, business. Bilingual but consume Arabic content emotionally and English content academically.
- **Secondary:** GCC pre-uni students (16–18) prepping Qudurat/Tahsili/SAT.
- **Tertiary:** Young professionals studying for CFA, PMP, ACCA, board exams, postgrad apps.
- **Mental model:** They already know StudyStream/Focusmate from TikTok and Reels. They want the same thing but *for them* — Arabic-native, culturally fluent, doesn't feel like a Western import.

---

## 3. Brand essence — pick ONE direction, not all five

The word **Hala** (هلا) is Gulf hospitality — *welcome, come in, sit down, have qahwa.* That's the soul of the brand. Build around it.

| Pillar | What it means visually |
|---|---|
| **Warm welcome** | Nobody gets shamed for procrastinating. The product *opens its door*. |
| **Quiet ambition** | These students have huge dreams (med school, KAUST, Ivies). Treat them seriously, not cutely. |
| **Late-night solidarity** | The 2am exam-week crew. Lamp light, qahwa, fairy lights, rain on windows. |
| **Bilingual confidence** | Arabic script is a design feature, not a translation layer. |
| **Modern Gulf, not Orientalist** | Riyadh in 2026, not "Aladdin." |

---

## 4. What we are NOT — competitive visual landscape

This is the most important section. Show the designer what to *avoid*:

- **StudyStream** → soft pastel, slightly childish, hero illustration feels like a kids' bedtime app. **Don't copy this. Out-class it.**
- **Focusmate** → corporate SaaS blue, sterile, looks like a Zoom alternative. **Too cold.**
- **Flow Club** → premium-aspirational but feels like a Soho House for white knowledge workers. **Wrong cultural register.**
- **Notion / Linear / Arc** → editorial, premium, refined. **Closer to what we want, but built for tech workers, not students.**
- **Most Arabic edtech** → cluttered, gradient-heavy, government-portal aesthetic. **Avoid completely.**

**The gap we're filling:** *Editorial-grade visual confidence (Notion/Arc tier) + Gulf cultural warmth + student-native energy.* That combination doesn't exist yet.

---

## 5. Mood direction

One sentence to anchor everything: **"A well-designed study lamp on a desk at 1am — quiet, focused, warm, and yours."**

Sensory references:
- Light: warm lamp glow, not fluorescent. Deep amber + soft cream.
- Texture: aged paper, brushed brass, dark walnut, condensation on a glass of iced karak.
- Sound (for motion/UX cues): turning a page, gentle chime, qahwa being poured. *Not* notification dings.
- Time of day: golden hour and late night, not midday.

---

## 6. Concrete design pillars to explore

Ask the designer to deliver **3 distinct theme directions**, each pushing one of these axes:

1. **"Archival Study"** — editorial, aged-paper, deep ink, brass accents. Premium, intellectual.
2. **"Late-Night Lo-Fi"** — dark mode native, warm amber glow, rain/window textures, Gulf night-sky palette.
3. **"Gulf Modernism"** — desert-meets-modernist palette (sand, indigo, terracotta, deep teal), Najdi-inspired geometric patterns used *sparingly* as accent, not theme.

We'll pick one and refine. Three is the right number — fewer = no real choice, more = analysis paralysis.

---

## 7. Bilingual / cultural requirements (non-negotiable)

- **Full RTL parity from day one.** Not retrofitted. Arabic and English should both feel like "the original."
- **Arabic typography is a first-class design choice.** Recommend: *IBM Plex Sans Arabic, Noto Sans Arabic, 29LT Bukra,* or *RTL Najd*. Latin pair should be a clean editorial sans (Inter, GT America, or similar).
- **Numerals:** support both Eastern Arabic (٠١٢٣) and Western Arabic (0123) — let users toggle. Default to Western for compatibility, but show Eastern in moments where it adds warmth (e.g., streak counter, timer).
- **Iconography:** avoid camels, lamps with genies, mosques, and other Orientalist tropes. Geometric Najdi-inspired patterns, qahwa imagery, modern Gulf architecture references are fair game when used with restraint.
- **No women's faces in marketing illustrations unless the team specifically approves** — this is a GCC market sensitivity. Use silhouettes, hands, objects, or stylized avatars instead.

UI kit details:

Your UI kits should be pixel-perfect recreations, created by reading the original UI code if possible, or using figma's get-design-context. They should be modular and reusable, so they can easily be pieced together for real designs. UI kits should recreate key screens in the product as click-thru prototypes. a UI kit's index.html must look like a typical view of the product. These are recreations, not storybooks.
